# Research Plan: AI Task Suitability - Additional Dimensions

## Main Question
What additional dimensions (beyond Paras Chopra's 3-axis framework) can help determine which tasks are suitable to delegate to AI?

## Core Framework from the Article
Paras Chopra proposes 3 orthogonal axes:
1. **Ease of building a verifier** (coding = easy, lab equipment = hard)
2. **Causal complexity** / number of confounds (math = low, startup success = high)
3. **Economic attractiveness** (coding = high, many domains = low)

## Subtopic 1: Existing AI Task Suitability Frameworks
Research other frameworks/models that classify tasks by AI suitability (e.g., HAI's AI Index, McKinsey's automation potential, task-technology fit models).

## Subtopic 2: Task Characteristics That Predict AI Readiness
What inherent characteristics of a task make it more/less suitable for AI? (e.g., data availability, decision frequency, stakes of failure, need for creativity, physical dexterity, social intelligence).

## Subtopic 3: Human-AI Complementarity Factors
Dimensions related to how humans and AI can best work together - what tasks benefit from augmentation vs. full automation?

## Synthesis
Combine all findings to produce an expanded dimensional framework for evaluating AI task suitability.
