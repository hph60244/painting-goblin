## 1. Current Taiwan Real Estate Market Trends (2025-2026)

### Market is Cooling Rapidly
- **National house prices fell 3.66% YoY in Q4 2025** (4.90% inflation-adjusted) — worst since Q1 2016
- **Housing transactions plummeted 25.5% YoY in 2025** to 261,000 units — lowest in 9 years
- **Jan-Feb 2026 transactions down 3.7% YoY** across 6 major cities (28,717 units)
- Market is in a clear downturn driven by central bank credit controls and tighter lending

### Regional Breakdown (Q4 2025 YoY Price Changes):
| City | Nominal Change | Real Change |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Taipei | -0.62% | -1.90% |
| New Taipei | -3.02% | -4.27% |
| Taoyuan | +0.59% | -0.70% |
| Hsinchu | -8.52% | -9.69% |
| Taichung | -6.79% | -7.98% |
| Tainan | -12.99% | -14.11% |
| Kaohsiung | -7.33% | -8.52% |

### Key Drivers
- **Central bank 7th round of selective credit controls** (Sept 2024): LTV for 2nd home reduced from 60% → 50% (raised to 60% in March 2026)
- **Grace period restrictions** for buyers with existing properties
- **LTV for 3rd+ properties and luxury homes**: reduced from 40% → 30%
- **Bank liquidity tightening** and stricter mortgage lending
- **Housing supply declining**: residential construction licenses fell 11.5% in 2025, and another ~30% in Jan-Feb 2026

### Economic Context
- Taiwan GDP grew 8.68% in 2025 (fastest since 2010), driven by AI chips and electronics exports
- 2026 GDP forecast: 7.71%
- Mortgage interest rate: ~2.34% (20-year fixed)
- House price-to-income ratio: 9.89 nationwide (2025), down from 10.76 in 2024
- Taipei remains severely unaffordable: price-to-income ratio of 15.41

### Outlook
- Housing market expected to remain subdued in 2026 until bank liquidity improves and credit controls ease
- Evertrust Rehouse forecasts 251,000–264,000 units sold in 2026 (possible 3% decline or 2% increase)
- **Buyer's market** — motivated sellers may accept lower offers

## 3. Factors That Affect Property Pricing in Taiwan

### Location Factors (Most Important)
- **City & District:** Major price differences exist between Taipei vs New Taipei vs Taoyuan vs other cities
- **MRT proximity:** Properties within 500m of MRT station command 10–20% premium
- **School district:** Top school districts command significant premium (especially in Taipei)
- **生活圈 (Living circle):** Availability of supermarkets, restaurants, parks, hospitals
- **Community reputation:** Developer brand, management quality, building maintenance

### Building & Unit Factors
- **Floor level:** Top floor commands premium (views, privacy); ground floor often discounted (noise, privacy)
- **Building age:** 5 years is near-new; major depreciation kicks in at 15–20 years
- **Total ping / layout:** 32.4 ping living area for 3BR is very comfortable in Taiwan (most 3BRs are 25–35 ping)
- **Parking:** Flat (平面) vs mechanical (機械) — significant value difference
- **Orientation:** South-facing units are preferred in Taiwan (avoid west-facing heat)
- **付屬建物 (Ancillary):** Balcony/terrace area (~11.1 ping here) — large balcony is a plus

### Market Cycle Factors
- **Current buyer's market** favors the purchaser — prices declining, more inventory available
- **Government policies:** Credit controls, luxury tax, and property gain tax (45% within 2 years, 35% 2–5 years) reduce speculation
- **Interest rates:** ~2.34% — still relatively low but rising

### Financial Factors
- **Price-to-income ratio:** 9.89 nationally, 15.41 in Taipei — affordability is a major constraint
- **Rental yield:** Very low (1.2–2.4%) — this is NOT a good investment property play
- **Loan-to-Value (LTV):** First home up to 80% LTV; second home currently 60% LTV (as of Mar 2026)

## 4. Red Flags & Things to Check When Buying a Resale Unit in Taiwan

### Legal & Title Checks
- **土地持分 (Land ownership share):** Verify the property's land hold is proportional and properly registered
- **Presale assignment restrictions:** New rules (2021) tax resales within 5 years at 35–45%
- **Property Gain Tax (房地合一稅 2.0):**
  - Sold within 2 years: 45% tax on gains
  - Sold between 2–5 years: 35% tax
  - 5–10 years: 20%
  - Over 10 years: 15%
  - First-time seller with gains < NT$4M: exempt
- **Confirm ownership is 住 (residential) vs 商 (commercial)** — affects taxes and usage

### Physical Inspection — Top Floor Specific
- **Roof waterproofing:** CHECK THOROUGHLY — top floor units are prone to leakage. 5-year-old building should still be within warranty (typically 5–15 years for structural, ask developer)
- **Water pressure:** Top floor may have lower water pressure; check if building has a pressurized water tank
- **Roof access/exclusive use:** Some top floor units come with exclusive roof usage rights — verify if this applies
- **Elevator maintenance:** Check elevator age and maintenance records; 20 floors means heavy elevator usage
- **Heat/cooling load:** Top floor gets more sun exposure — verify air conditioning adequacy

### Community & Management
- **Management committee (管委會):** Ask about financial reserves, planned maintenance, recent assessments
- **Management fee:** NT$9,300/quarter = NT$3,100/month — reasonable for ~45 ping at ~NT$70/ping/month, which is standard
- **Building usage ratio (公設比):** If ~45.4 total vs ~32.6 main + ~11.1 附属, then common area = 45.4 - 32.6 - 11.1 = 1.7 ping. This seems very low — verify the breakdown
- **公設比 calculation:** Total = main + 附属 + common. If only 1.7 ping is common, the 公設比 is extremely low (~3.7%). Most modern buildings are 28–35%. This needs verification — the numbers may not add up.

### Parking Verification
- **Parking space registration:** Confirm the parking space is a registered independent property right (independent产权) or a use-right only (使用權)
- **Flat parking:** Confirm dimensions — some "flat" spaces are actually cramped
- **EV charging:** Check if building allows EV charger installation in parking space

### Transaction Process
- **實價登錄 (Actual Price Registration):** Check recent transaction records for this building/community on 實價登錄网站
- **Housing inspection (房屋檢查):** Hire a professional inspector for structural, electrical, and plumbing checks
- **履約保證 (Escrow):** Use a履约保证 service to protect deposit funds during transaction
- **Negotiation leverage:** With market cooling, aggressively negotiate — check how long the unit has been listed

### General Taiwan Buying Tips
- **Foreign buyers:** Additional approvals and restrictions apply; land ownership restricted near military zones
- **Flood/earthquake risk:** Check if the area is in a flood zone or near a fault line
- **Fung shui (風水):** Many Taiwanese buyers factor in fung shui — top floor corner unit usually scores well
- **Renovation costs:** Mid-range decoration may need updating in 3–5 years — budget NT$1M–2M for a full refresh
