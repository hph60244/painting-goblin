- Public facility ratio: **30.97%**
- **No gym, no swimming pool** — minimal facilities, keeping management costs lower

### 30.97% 公設比 Analysis

For a 7-floor building with 18 units:
- 30.97% is **slightly above average for the "華廈" (apartment/condo) category** in Taiwan, which typically ranges 25-30%
- However, for a small building with only 7 floors, the elevator shaft, stairwell, and lobby alone consume significant shared space
- By comparison: large-scale developments in the area (e.g. 竹科主人7 at 42.5 萬/ping) often have similar or higher ratios
- The ratio is **reasonable** given the single-elevator configuration and small footprint (209 ping)

## 6. Small Community (18 戶) — Pros & Cons Analysis

Based on PTT/Dcard discussions and expert commentary:

### Pros (優勢)
1. **單純居住環境** — Only 18 households, low population density, fewer strangers
2. **低公設 = 低管理費潛力** — Basic facilities only (meeting room, mailbox), no expensive amenities like pools or gyms to maintain
3. **出入單純** — You'll know your neighbors; easier security management
4. **停車位 1:1** — Every unit gets a dedicated parking space; no competition
5. **電梯等候時間短** — Only 3 units per floor, minimal wait for elevator
6. **建商系列推案** — Builder has proven track record with this product line

### Cons (劣勢)
1. **管理費負擔** — With only 18 units to split costs, monthly management fees could be **high per household** if 24/7 security guard is desired
2. **No 24-hour security** — Likely unable to afford 24-hour concierge; probably buzzer/intercom only or limited-hours security
3. **管委會運作挑戰** — Fewer people willing to serve as committee members; quorum issues may arise
4. **公設維修基金有限** — Emergency repairs for elevator or roof may require large special assessments
5. **轉手市場較小** — Only 18 units means fewer comparable sales; potential buyers may be limited
6. **無豐富公設** — No gym, children's play area, or garden — may be a con for families with kids

### PTT Community Consensus (from home-sale board):
> "戶數少於50戶的社區缺點非常明顯：管理費高、不能請24小時警衛、公設有問題很難修繕"
> "100~200戶為黃金區段" — 70-200 units considered optimal by most PTT users
> "戶數少 單純 未來賣屋沒對手" — Some argue low unit count = less selling competition

Source: [PTT home-sale - 戶數多的社區優缺點](https://www.ptt.cc/bbs/home-sale/M.1735745101.A.C85.html)
Source: [StockFeel - 社區戶數優缺點分析](https://www.stockfeel.com.tw/%E7%A4%BE%E5%8D%80%E6%88%B6%E6%95%B8-%E6%88%B6%E6%95%B8%E5%A4%9A-%E6%88%B6%E6%95%B8%E5%B0%91-%E5%84%AA%E7%BC%BA%E9%BB%9E-%E8%B2%B7%E6%88%BF-%E6%8C%91%E6%88%BF/)

## 9. Summary Assessment

### Strengths
- ✅ Proven developer (8th phase in the series)
- ✅ Nearly sold out — strong market validation
- ✅ 1:1 parking ratio with EV pre-wiring
- ✅ Quality fit-out brands (Rinnai, Electrolux, TOTO)
- ✅ Good layout features (dual-facing, bathroom windows)
- ✅ Located in established neighborhood near Science Park

### Concerns
- ⚠️ **18 units is very small** — PTT consensus says 70-200 is ideal; 18 is at the extreme low end for management cost sharing
- ⚠️ **30.97% public ratio is on the higher side** for such basic facilities — check whether the ratio is justified
- ⚠️ **No 24-hour security** likely — important for security-conscious buyers
- ⚠️ **Potential for high per-unit special assessments** when elevator or roof needs major repair
- ⚠️ **Limited resale pool** — only 18 units means fewer future listings and potentially harder to sell

### Recommended Due Diligence Questions
1. What is the expected monthly management fee per ping?
2. Is 24-hour security planned or just intercom/buzzer?
3. What is the projected monthly management fee budget and how does it split across 18 units?
4. Is there a management reserve fund plan for major repairs (elevator, roof waterproofing)?
5. Are there any restrictions on short-term rentals (Airbnb, etc.)?
