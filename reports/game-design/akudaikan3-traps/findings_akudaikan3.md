# 悪代官3 (Akudaikan 3) — Research Findings

**Date:** 2026-06-06
**Primary Source:** Japanese Wikipedia — 悪代官 (ゲーム) (https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%82%AA%E4%BB%A3%E5%AE%98_(%E3%82%B2%E3%83%BC%E3%83%A0))
**Note:** Most other sources (GameFAQs, GiantBomb, MobyGames, atwiki) returned 403/404 errors.

## Overview

- **Release:** 2007-03-01, PlayStation 2, developed by Global A Entertainment
- **Genre change:** "Jidaigeki Simulation" (1&2) → "Jidaigeki Comedy Action Game" (3)
- **Directorial intent (Jetboy Morii):** "The protagonist is out of shape and pathetically weak" / "traps should look fun to trigger" / "a stupid game you laugh at from the outside"
- **Popularity:** Highest in the series; expensive on the used market as of 2026
- **Reboot:** Story is a complete reboot — not connected to 1 or 2

---

## Gameplay Differences: Akudaikan 3 vs 1&2

### 1. Camera & Graphics
- **1&2:** Top-down 2D view
- **3:** Full 3D camera, significantly improved graphics

### 2. Stage Structure
- **1&2:** Multiple distinct stages/locations (Edo, Egypt, Black Ship, etc.)
- **3:** Entire game takes place in the magistrate's mansion → no stage variety, but instead mansion expansion system

### 3. Trap System (BIG CHANGE)
- **1&2:** Preparation phase before battle — place traps within a time limit, then heroes arrive
- **3:** No preparation phase. Heroes arrive immediately when the stage starts. Traps can be placed AND activated in real-time, anywhere, anytime. Limit of 3 traps per room at a time. Each trap has a cooldown before activation.

### 4. Bodyguard System
- **1&2:** Bodyguards placed during prep phase, stay in position, only vassals follow the magistrate
- **3:** Bodyguards summoned on-demand per room. Up to 5 per room. Summon repeatedly as long as you have funds. Defeated bodyguards drop coins that replenish your current funds.

### 5. Equipment Upgrades
- **2:** Traps and bodyguards could be upgraded (using gunbai / screws)
- **3:** NO trap/bodyguard upgrades. Instead: **mansion expansion system** (増築)

### 6. Item System
- **2:** Hidden items on stages, equipment weight affecting movement speed
- **3:** No hidden items on stages

### 7. Difficulty/Replay
- **2:** Star-based difficulty unlock system, binago puzzles
- **3:** Multi-scenario, multi-ending branching (A through E routes). Rating from 0-10 stars instead of 0-5

### 8. Boss "Hell" Judgment
- **1&2:** All defeated enemies get Hell judgment
- **3:** Only the final boss-tier enemy gets Hell judgment

### 9. Minigames
- **2:** 「くの一取り調べ」 (memory card matching game)
- **3:** 「くのいち吟味」 (tickling game with a feather in Azami's armpit)
- **3 new:** 「清三がゆく」 (endless enemy slashing), 「負けるな恵比寿屋」 (coin collection dodge game)

## NEW in Akudaikan 3 — Unique Mechanics

### 10. "屈辱系" (Humiliation Traps)
A brand new trap category introduced in 3. These deal little to no HP damage but inflict **humiliation** on enemies. Enemies who take enough humiliation become "broken" (心が折れ / heart broken) and will:
- Retreat
- Commit suicide
- Surrender (regardless of remaining HP)

Some defeated enemies may even ask to be hired as bodyguards after their spirit is broken.

**Examples of humiliation weapons (noted in the article):**
- ピコピコハンマー (pico-pico hammer)
- 豆鉄砲 (bean shooter)
- 罵倒用語集 (insult dictionary / taunt)

### 11. "持病" (Chronic Illness)
The magistrate in 3 has a heart condition. Running continuously puts strain on his heart (shown by a face icon at the top of the screen). If the strain reaches maximum, he gets winded and cannot move for a period of time. Walking slowly or standing still recovers it.

This can be toggled off in options (for a healthy playthrough), but it affects stage rating.

### 12. "屋敷の増築" (Mansion Expansion)
Since all stages are in the magistrate's mansion, you can spend development funds to build new rooms, including:
- 草庵 (thatched hut)
- 大浴場 (large bath — with shower!)
- 拷問部屋 (torture room)
- Modern/Western rooms
- SF-style labs

**Room-specific traps:** Many rooms have unique built-in traps that trigger under certain conditions without needing to be manually placed. Some instantly kill enemies (e.g., dropping them from the 2nd floor, 雪隠流し, 石抱, 木馬責め).

### 13. "耐性" (Resistances)
Enemies in 3 have elemental/type **resistances**. If you attack with a resisted trap type, they automatically dodge AND teleport to a distant position, breaking your combo chain.

### 14. Combo Timer Extension
- **1&2:** Fixed timer between combo hits
- **3:** Timer can be extended by using immobilization traps, poison, or other status effects, or by building a longer chain

### 15. "ワナワナ君" (Wana-wana-kun)
In-universe device developed by Ebisuya that allows the magistrate to place and trigger traps anytime, anywhere. Three buttons corresponding to the controller.

### 16. Bodyguard Hires from Defeated Enemies
Unique to 3: Some "broken" enemies can be recruited as bodyguards. Hired versions have dramatically lower HP than when they were enemies. Contract/employment fees can be exorbitant.

### 17. "格付け" (Titles) Unlocks Traps
- **1&2:** Titles are for rating only
- **3:** Certain specific titles are required to unlock development of certain traps

## Routes (Scenarios)

- **A Route:** "Stylish evil" — RPG-style heroes (勇者希望の少年, 魔法少女) attack
- **B Route:** Overthrow the shogunate — fight 徳川吉宗 (the "rampage shogun")
- **C Route:** Daughter Oyu begs father to stop evil — ends with fire rescue
- **D Route:** Wife Ochou betrays magistrate with Ebisuya — final boss is the wife
- **E Route (true final):** イナズマ帝国 (Inazuma Empire) army attacks — final boss イナズマ大元帥 → イナズマ大神王
