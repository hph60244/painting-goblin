## 1. Official Derivative Works Guidelines (Cover Corp.)

**Primary URL:** https://hololivepro.com/en/terms/

Cover Corp. maintains a comprehensive set of Derivative Works Guidelines covering multiple categories:
- Overall Guidelines (applicable to all derivative works)
- Guidelines for Derivative Work Games (fan games)
- Guidelines for Clips
- Fan Support Advertisement Guidelines
- Guidelines for Use of Music

### Key Points from the Overall Guidelines

- **No permission needed** for works that comply with the guidelines: *"When creating derivative works that comply with these overall guidelines, there is no need to contact us for permission."*
- **Hobby/fan level only:** *"Please limit your creation of derivative works to a fan or hobby level. Do not use our content for business purposes...or for purposes that can be deemed as for-profit."*
- **Definition of derivative works:** *"We consider derivative works to be creations born of fans' ideas and creativity, based on content created by us."*
- **What is NOT covered:** *"We do not consider the use of our content as is, or with modifications lacking creative input, to be classified as derivative works. Such use does not fall within the scope of these guidelines."*
- **Copyright retained:** *"We do not relinquish our copyright or related rights through these overall guidelines. We maintain these rights."*
- **No guarantees:** *"We make no guarantees in regards to the use of our content or related derivative works, including any actions that may violate a third party's rights."*
- **Prohibited content:**
  - Content falsely represented as official
  - Content contrary to public order and morality
  - Content involving ideology, belief, religion, or politics
  - Content damaging Cover's image, talents, or content
  - Content damaging third parties or violating their rights

### FAQ Confirmation
**URL:** https://hololivepro.com/en/question/

The FAQ explicitly confirms: *"For hololive production derivative works, please read and follow our 'Derivative Works Guidelines'."* (referencing the same URL above).

## 2. Specific Guidelines for Derivative Work Games

**URL:** https://hololivepro.com/en/terms/ (scroll to "Guidelines for Derivative Work Games" section)

### Critical Rules for Fan Games

1. **Additional Request:** When using third-party materials (fonts, images, BGM, music, textures, source code, etc.) in derivative work games, you must comply with the terms of use of those materials.

2. **No infringement:** *"Please do not infringe the copyrights, property rights, or any other rights of third parties, even if the material is your own work."*

### The holo Indie / Game Creator Support Program

This is the **only official path to monetize a fan game** using Hololive IP:

- **Purpose:** *"In order to co-create with fans and creators around the world, the 'Game Creator Support Program' has been created...to provide an ecosystem that supports ongoing activities from derivative work game creators."*
- **How it works:** Derivative work games are published on Steam under the **"holo Indie"** game brand.
- **Application:** via CCMC Corp. at https://ccmc-corp.com/en (entry form: https://ccmc-corp.com/en/entry/)
- **Screening:** *"All listings are subject to a screening process. As a result of the screening process, applications may be rejected at our discretion."*
- **Can be taken down:** *"Even after a derivative work game has been posted, the derivative work game may be withdrawn without notice if CCMC Corp. or we deem the listing to be inappropriate."*
- **Corporation/circle name use:** *"Only game creators who use the 'Game Creator Support Program'...will be permitted to create fan made games under a corporation's or circle's name."*

### Key Monetization Rule (from holo Indie game descriptions)

Every holo Indie game's Steam page includes this disclaimer:
> *"This game is allowed to be monetized under the examination and terms of 'holo Indie'. Please note that any monetized derivative work games that include Cover Corp.'s content outside of holo Indie is not covered by these terms and is not allowed."*

**This means:** If you want to charge money for a Hololive fan game, you **must** go through the holo Indie program. Free fan games outside holo Indie are permitted under the hobby-level rules of the overall guidelines.

## 3. Specific Rules About Voice Clips from Streams

### Clip Guidelines (within the Derivative Works Guidelines)

**URL:** https://hololivepro.com/en/terms/ (scroll to "Guidelines for Clips" section)

- **Clips must credit the original source:**
  - Include a URL link to the original source stream/video
  - Include the source's title
- **No clipping before VOD is public:** *"Please refrain from uploading clips before the source stream ends and the VOD is made public."*
- **Paid content is OFF-LIMITS:** *"The clip and overall guidelines do not apply to clips featuring paid content, i.e. members-only videos, concert footage, etc. We forbid the use of such content unless express permission is granted beforehand."*
- **Monetization of clips is allowed** (e.g. YouTube Partner Program), but:
  - You **cannot** register clips with Content ID as your own work.
  - All terms of use and third-party guidelines must be followed.

### Implications for Voice Clips in Games

- **Extracting voice clips from VODs for use in a game is NOT explicitly covered** by the clip guidelines, which are written for video clips posted to sharing sites.
- The **overall guidelines** apply: such use would need to be "fan or hobby level" and not-for-profit.
- The **music guidelines explicitly forbid AI voice extraction:** *"We do not allow extraction of our talents' voices from our songs to be used in speech generation, and do not consider this to be derivative work."*
- **Members-only/paid content voices are strictly forbidden** from being used.
- **Concerts/paid events footage** — voice from these sources is prohibited.

**Bottom line:** Using voice clips from free/public stream VODs in a non-commercial fan game appears to be tolerated under the hobby-level provision, but it exists in a gray area — the guidelines do not explicitly address "sampling stream voices into a game binary." The holo Indie program offers the safest path if you want to use voices in a released game.

## 4. Official Voice Packs or Licensed Sources

There are **no official voice clip packs or sound libraries** available for fan game creators to license directly. The available options are:

1. **holo Indie program** (https://ccmc-corp.com/en/holoindie/) — The official pipeline for publishing fan games. Approved creators presumably receive guidance on what assets they can use. This is the only way to legally sell a Hololive fan game with voice clips.

2. **piapro page** (https://piapro.jp/hololive) — For **music only** (covers, instrumentals). This provides sound source files for original Hololive songs, NOT voice clips of talents talking. Monetization is not available when using these files.

3. **YouTube Shorts / TikTok libraries** — Hololive music can be used through these platforms' registered libraries with monetization enabled, but this is for short-form video content, not games.

4. **Licensed commercial games** — There are officially licensed Hololive games (e.g., "Okayunyumu! R", "Holo's Hanafuda", "Juufuutei Raden's Guide for Pixel Museum") but these are done through formal licensing agreements, not something available to indie creators.

## 6. Summary of What Is and Isn't Permitted

### ✅ Permitted (without holo Indie)
- Creating a **free** fan game at hobby level with original assets inspired by Hololive
- Using Hololive talents' names and likenesses in a respectful, fan-made context
- Using official music from piapro for covers/remixes (non-commercial)
- Using clip-style content from public VODs with proper attribution (for video content)

### ❌ Not Permitted
- **Selling** a fan game without going through holo Indie
- Using **members-only, concert, or paid content** audio in any form
- Using AI to extract/replicate talents' voices (explicitly banned in music guidelines)
- Registering derivative content with Content ID as your own work
- Creating content that could be mistaken for official
- Using Hololive IP **as-is** without creative transformation

### ⚠️ Gray Areas
- Extracting voice clips from public VODs into a game binary — not explicitly addressed, but tolerated under holo Indie (see HoRoyal)
- Using fan-translated clips — may involve third-party rights issues
- Commercial use outside holo Indie but not directly selling the game (e.g., Patreon, donations) — likely violates the "not for business purposes" clause

### ✅ The Safe Commercial Path: holo Indie Program
1. Apply at https://ccmc-corp.com/en/entry/
2. Game is screened by CCMC Corp.
3. If approved, published on Steam under the holo Indie brand
4. Monetization is explicitly permitted under the program terms
5. Voice clip usage (sourced from public streams) has precedent via HoRoyal
